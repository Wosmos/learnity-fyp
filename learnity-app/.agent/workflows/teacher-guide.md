# Teacher's Guide: How to Use Learnity

## Getting Started

### 1. Application Process

- Visit the Learnity website and click "Become a Teacher"
- Fill out the application form with your credentials
- Wait for admin approval (you'll receive an email notification)
- Once approved, you can access your teacher dashboard

### 2. Setting Up Your Profile

- Go to Dashboard → Profile
- Complete your profile with:
  - Professional photo
  - Bio and teaching experience
  - Qualifications and certifications
  - Subjects you teach
  - Hourly rate (if applicable)
  - Availability schedule

## Creating and Managing Courses

### 3. Creating a New Course

1. Navigate to Dashboard → Courses
2. Click "Create Course" button
3. Fill in course details:
   - Title and description
   - Category and difficulty level
   - Tags for discoverability
   - Thumbnail image (optional)
4. Click "Save as Draft"

### 4. Adding Course Content

1. Open your course from the Courses page
2. Click "Edit Course"
3. Create Sections:
   - Sections organize your lessons into modules
   - Example: "Introduction to Algebra", "Advanced Concepts"
4. Add Lessons to each section:
   - **Video Lessons**: Paste YouTube URL or upload video
   - **Text Lessons**: Write content using the editor
   - **Quiz Lessons**: Add questions with multiple choice answers
5. Set lesson order by dragging and dropping

### 5. Creating Quizzes

1. Within a lesson, click "Add Quiz"
2. Set quiz parameters:
   - Title and description
   - Passing score (percentage)
   - Time limit (optional)
3. Add questions:
   - Write the question
   - Add 4 answer options
   - Mark the correct answer
   - Set points for each question

### 6. Publishing Your Course

**Before publishing, ensure:**

- Course has at least 3 lessons
- All sections have clear titles
- Video URLs are working
- Quizzes have correct answers marked

**To publish:**

1. Go to Dashboard → Courses
2. Click the menu (three dots) on your course
3. Select "Publish"
4. Your course will now be visible to students!

## Managing Students

### 7. Viewing Enrolled Students

- Go to Dashboard → Courses → [Your Course] → Students
- See list of all enrolled students
- View their progress and completion rates
- See quiz scores and performance

### 8. Tracking Student Progress

- Dashboard shows overall student statistics
- Individual student pages show:
  - Lessons completed
  - Quiz scores
  - Time spent learning
  - Last activity date

## Understanding Your Dashboard

### 9. Dashboard Metrics

Your dashboard displays:

- **Total Students**: Number of students enrolled in your courses
- **Published Courses**: Courses visible to students
- **Total Enrollments**: Sum of all course enrollments
- **Average Rating**: Your overall teacher rating from reviews

### 10. Analytics

- View course performance metrics
- Track enrollment trends
- See which lessons are most popular
- Monitor student completion rates

## Video Management

### 11. Adding Videos to Lessons

Learnity stores **YouTube video URLs**, not direct video files.

**To add a video:**

1. Upload your video to YouTube (can be unlisted)
2. Copy the video URL
3. In your lesson editor, paste the YouTube URL
4. The system will automatically extract and embed the video
5. Set video duration for tracking purposes

**Accepted formats:**

- Full YouTube URL: `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VIDEO_ID`
- Short URL: `https://youtu.be/VIDEO_ID`
- Embed URL: `https://www.youtube.com/embed/VIDEO_ID`

## Revenue and Earnings

### 12. Setting Course Pricing

- During course creation, set if course is Free or Paid
- If paid, set the price
- View earnings in Dashboard → Earnings
- Track revenue per course

### 13. Payment and Withdrawals

- Payments are processed monthly
- View pending and completed payments
- Update bank details in Profile → Payment Settings

## Best Practices

### 14. Creating Engaging Content

- **Start with clear learning objectives**
- **Break complex topics into smaller lessons**
- **Use a mix of video, text, and quizzes**
- **Keep videos under 15 minutes** for better engagement
- **Add quizzes after every 2-3 lessons** to reinforce learning
- **Respond to student questions** promptly
- **Update content** based on student feedback

### 15. Improving Course Visibility

- Use descriptive, searchable titles
- Add relevant tags
- Write detailed course descriptions
- Create eye-catching thumbnails
- Encourage satisfied students to leave reviews
- Maintain a high course completion rate

### 17. Common Issues

**Q: My videos aren't displaying**

- A: Ensure YouTube URL is correct and video is not private

**Q: Students can't see my course**

- A: Check that course status is "Published", not "Draft"

**Q: How do I delete a course?**

- A: Courses with enrolled students cannot be deleted. Unpublish instead.

**Q: Can I edit a published course?**

- A: Yes! Changes save immediately. Students will see updated content.
